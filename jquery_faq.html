National Park Facts

National Park Service
protects over 84 million acres of wild landscapes and historic sites.
Yellowstone National Park
the oldest U.S. national park, founded in 1872.
Mackinac National Park
established in 1875 and was returned to the state in 1895. It is now one of Michigan's State Parks.
Ribbon Falls
nine times taller than Niagara Falls.
Death Valley National Park
One of the hottest temperatures on earth was recorded in 1913, registering 134 degrees.
Grand Canyon National Park
The Grand Canyon is known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.
Denali National Park
Mount McKinley in the Alaska Range is the highest point in the national parks at 20,302 feet.
California's Sequoia National Park
home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world, the wonderfully named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275 feet tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tons.
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park
the largest national park, covering 13,000 square miles or 13.2 million acres.
Great Smoky Mountains National Park
the most visited national park with more than 9 million guests per year, followed by Grand Canyon National Park, which gets more than 4 million visits per year.
